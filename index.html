Lyric Timestamps

Pick a song and paste its lyrics (Suno format is fine — lines in [brackets] become instrumental sections). The cloud aligns them to the audio and saves a -timestamps.txt next to the MP3 in Dropbox. Runs on CPU, so give it a few minutes. (At home, HTR-Menu #22 on your GPU does the same thing faster.)
Opens the editor with the song's saved timestamps (if any).

"Time manually" skips the cloud aligner: your pasted lyrics open in the editor exactly as written (brackets become section markers), and you stamp each line against the audio — the most exact option, and the words are always right.
.srt imports into Filmora/CapCut/Premiere as clean, correct captions — no speech-to-text. In Filmora: delete any auto-captions, then File → Import → Import Media, pick the .srt, drag it onto the timeline.

Timestamp Editor

Play the song, click a line to select it, and hit Stamp (or press Enter) the instant it's sung — the line takes the current playback time and selection moves to the next line. Space = play/pause. Times are precise to a tenth of a second. Section markers are separators (never captions) and show how long each section runs.

Lyric Video — backgrounds & render

Upload 4–8 backgrounds (playback order: intro → verse 1 block → verse 2 → bridge → final). They're stored with the song. Rendering runs in the cloud — the finished 16:9 + 9:16 MP4s land in the song's Video Files folder in Dropbox after a few minutes. Needs saved timestamps (use the Timestamp Editor above first).
Each cue = the moment a background starts. Images are picked by their number, so the same image can appear more than once (start AND end on the cover). Numbers refer to each format's own set. Without cues, backgrounds follow song sections automatically.
